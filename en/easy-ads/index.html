Why Traditional Advertising Often Doesn't Last

Most advertising campaigns are designed to generate immediate enquiries. The customer sees an advert, clicks, writes a message or makes a purchase.

Real buying decisions rarely happen that way. People usually need time to notice a business, understand what it offers, see it several times and gradually build trust.

  • One advertising impression is rarely enough.
  • Customers compare several companies before deciding.
  • Familiar businesses inspire more confidence.
  • People often buy much later, when they actually need the service.
  • One-time campaigns quickly lose momentum once advertising stops.

The problem is often not the advertising itself. The problem is that businesses appear too rarely and try to sell too early.
